Using genetics and labs to guide mood treatment.

Not all antidepressants work the same for everyone. Genetics help explain why.

By testing for key variants, we can often predict which medications may be more effective or better tolerated—helping you get to the right treatment faster.

Labs also check for thyroid, vitamin, or other medical issues that can mimic depression. Together, these tools reduce uncertainty and help us avoid unnecessary trial-and-error.

Common Questions

Isn't depression usually treated by psychiatrists?

Mild to moderate depression is most often first evaluated and managed by internists and primary care doctors. That's the standard of care. At MoodPath MD, we take that medical foundation further—with more time, targeted labs, and genetic testing—to guide treatment more precisely. For more complex cases, we collaborate with psychiatry when needed.

Is this different from seeing my PCP or a psychiatrist?

Yes. PCPs often have limited time, and psychiatrists may not review your full medical picture. We combine both: internist expertise + psychiatric care + genetics to guide safer, faster treatment.

How effective is this approach?

In large studies, genetic-guided prescribing improved response and remission rates compared to standard trial-and-error. Results vary, but this approach gives us more clarity from the start.

How much time will this save me?

Most patients complete labs and the initial consult within a week, versus months of back-and-forth elsewhere. Visits are telehealth—no commutes or waiting rooms.

Why pay out-of-pocket when psychiatry may be covered?

Traditional care often means short visits and months of trial-and-error. Our model reduces wasted time, side effects, and visits—often saving money and productivity in the long run.

Do I need a PCP?

Yes. Every patient must have a PCP for general health. We focus on mood, while your PCP manages your broader care.
