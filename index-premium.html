Frequently Asked Questions

Isn't depression usually treated by psychiatrists?

Mild to moderate depression is most often first evaluated and managed by internists and primary care doctors. That's the standard of care. We expand on this foundation with more time, targeted labs, and genetic testing to guide treatment more precisely.

How is this different from my regular doctor?

PCPs often have limited time (15-minute visits). We offer 90-minute consultations, comprehensive medical workups, and genetic testing to personalize your treatment. This thoroughness often reduces trial-and-error from months to weeks.

What about insurance?

We're currently out-of-network. Many patients use HSA/FSA funds or submit for partial reimbursement. We provide detailed superbills for insurance submission. Labs are often covered when ordered by your PCP.

Do I need a primary care provider?

Yes, we require patients to have a PCP for comprehensive care coordination and medical emergencies. We work collaboratively with your PCP and can help you find one if needed.

How does genetic testing help?

Your DNA reveals how your body metabolizes different antidepressants. This helps predict which medications will work best and which might cause side effects, reducing the typical trial-and-error approach.

What if I need immediate help?

For crisis situations, call 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) or go to your nearest emergency room. We provide scheduled care for non-emergency mental health needs and can typically see new patients within a week.
