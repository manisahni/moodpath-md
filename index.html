Common Questions

Isn't depression usually treated by psychiatrists?

Mild to moderate depression is most often first evaluated and managed by internists and primary care doctors. That's the standard of care. As a board-certified internist with specialized training in depression and CBT, I expand on this foundation with more time, targeted labs, and evidence-based treatment approaches.

Do I need a primary care provider?

Yes, we require patients to have a PCP for comprehensive care coordination and medical emergencies. We work collaboratively with your PCP to ensure you receive the best possible care.

How is this different from traditional therapy?

I provide comprehensive medical treatment for mild-moderate depression, combining medication management with CBT techniques. This integrated approach addresses both the biological and psychological aspects of depression, offering a more complete treatment solution than medication or therapy alone.
