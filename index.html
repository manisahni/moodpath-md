Common Questions

Isn't depression usually treated by psychiatrists?

Mild to moderate depression is most often first evaluated and managed by internists and primary care doctors. That's the standard of care. While psychiatrists typically handle severe cases, board-certified internists like myself are well-qualified to treat mild-moderate depression. As a board-certified internist with strong interest in depression and CBT, I expand on this foundation with more time, targeted labs, and evidence-based treatment approaches.

Do I need a primary care provider?

Yes, we require patients to have a PCP for comprehensive care coordination and medical emergencies. We work collaboratively with your PCP to ensure you receive the best possible care.

How is this different from traditional therapy?

I provide comprehensive medical treatment for mild-moderate depression, combining medication management with CBT techniques. This integrated approach addresses both the biological and psychological aspects of depression, offering a more complete treatment solution than medication or therapy alone. While psychiatrists and therapists focus on specific aspects, I provide board-certified medical care with integrated CBT approaches.

Should I see a psychiatrist or an internist for depression?

For mild to moderate depression, board-certified internists are often the first choice and standard of care. Psychiatrists typically handle severe cases, complex medication regimens, or treatment-resistant depression. As a board-certified internist with strong interest in depression and CBT, I'm well-qualified to treat mild-moderate depression and can refer to psychiatrists if needed for more complex cases.
