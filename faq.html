Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about MoodPath MD

Getting Started

How do I know if MoodPath MD is right for me?
Take our 2-minute fit check! It will ask about your symptoms, treatment history, and current situation. Based on your responses, we'll let you know if we can help or if you'd be better served by a different type of care.
How quickly can I get an appointment?
Most patients can be seen within 3-5 business days. After completing the fit check and any needed labs, you'll have access to our scheduling system to choose a time that works for you.
What happens in the first visit?
The 90-minute initial consultation includes: comprehensive mood assessment, complete medical history review, discussion of your goals, explanation of treatment options (including genetic testing), and creation of a personalized treatment plan.

Our Approach

Isn't depression usually treated by psychiatrists?
Mild to moderate depression is most often first evaluated and managed by internists and primary care doctors. That's the standard of care. At MoodPath MD, we take that medical foundation further—with more time, targeted labs, and genetic testing—to guide treatment more precisely. For more complex cases, we collaborate with psychiatry when needed.
How is this different from traditional therapy?
We provide medical treatment for depression and anxiety, including medication management and genetic testing. While therapy focuses on talk therapy, we focus on the medical aspects of mental health care. Many patients benefit from both our medical treatment and traditional therapy.
What conditions do you treat?
We focus on low mood, dysthymia, and mild to moderate depression. We also address related concerns like anxiety that often accompanies depression. We use medical expertise to rule out underlying causes like thyroid issues or vitamin deficiencies.

Practical Questions

Do I need a primary care provider (PCP)?
Yes, we require patients to have a PCP for comprehensive care coordination and medical emergencies. We work collaboratively with your PCP to ensure you receive the best possible care. If you don't have a PCP, we can help you find one.
How much time will this save me?
Most patients complete labs and the initial consult within a week, versus months of back-and-forth elsewhere. Visits are telehealth—no commutes or waiting rooms. Genetic testing can help you avoid weeks or months of trying medications that won't work for your body.
Is this private and confidential?
Yes. Visits are confidential and HIPAA-compliant. Records are not shared with your employer. We only share information with your PCP (with permission) to ensure coordinated care.

Cost & Insurance

How much does treatment cost?
Initial consultation: $249 (one-time). Monthly membership: $89/month for ongoing care. Genetic testing: $399 (optional, one-time). We provide superbills for insurance reimbursement.
Do you accept insurance?
We don't bill insurance directly, but we provide superbills you can submit to PPO insurance. Many patients get 40–70% back. HSA/FSA funds can also be used for our services.
Why pay out-of-pocket when psychiatry may be covered?
Traditional care often means short visits and months of trial-and-error. Our model reduces wasted time, side effects, and visits—often saving money and productivity in the long run. Many patients find the investment pays for itself through faster recovery and fewer medication failures.

About Genetic Testing

How does genetic testing help with depression treatment?
Your genes affect how you metabolize medications. Testing reveals which antidepressants your body processes normally, slowly, or quickly—helping predict which will work best and at what dose. This reduces trial-and-error and side effects.
Is genetic testing required?
No, it's optional. However, about 80% of our patients choose it because it provides valuable, lifelong guidance for medication selection—useful not just for depression but any future medication needs.
How is the test done?
It's a simple cheek swab you do at home. We mail you the kit, you swab your cheek, and mail it back. Results are available in 7-10 days.
