Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about MoodPath MD

Treatment Approach

Isn't depression usually treated by psychiatrists?
Mild to moderate depression is most often first evaluated and managed by internists and primary care doctors. That's the standard of care. At MoodPath MD, we take that medical foundation further—with more time, targeted labs, and genetic testing—to guide treatment more precisely. For more complex cases, we collaborate with psychiatry when needed.
Is this different from seeing my PCP or a psychiatrist?
Yes. PCPs often have limited time (15-minute visits), and psychiatrists may not review your full medical picture. We combine both: internist expertise + psychiatric care + genetics to guide safer, faster treatment. Our 90-minute initial consultation allows us to thoroughly understand your situation.
How effective is this approach?
In large studies, genetic-guided prescribing improved response and remission rates compared to standard trial-and-error. Results vary, but this approach gives us more clarity from the start, often reducing the time to find an effective medication from months to weeks.
What conditions do you treat?
We focus on low mood, dysthymia, and mild to moderate depression. We also address related concerns like anxiety that often accompanies depression. We use medical expertise to rule out underlying causes like thyroid issues or vitamin deficiencies.

Practical Concerns

How much time will this save me?
Most patients complete labs and the initial consult within a week, versus months of back-and-forth elsewhere. Visits are telehealth—no commutes or waiting rooms. Genetic testing can help you avoid weeks or months of trying medications that won't work for your body.
Do I have to manage labs on my own?
No. We send lab orders to Quest or LabCorp near you. You simply show up for the blood draw. Results are shared with you, and—with your permission—your PCP. We handle all the coordination.
Is this private?
Yes. Visits are confidential and HIPAA-compliant. Records are not shared with your employer. We only share information with your PCP (with permission) to ensure coordinated care.
What technology do I need?
Just a computer, tablet, or smartphone with internet and a camera. We use secure, HIPAA-compliant video platforms that work in your browser—no special software needed.

Cost & Insurance

Why pay out-of-pocket when psychiatry may be covered?
Traditional care often means short visits and months of trial-and-error. Our model reduces wasted time, side effects, and visits—often saving money and productivity in the long run. Many patients find the investment pays for itself through faster recovery and fewer medication failures.
Can I get reimbursed?
Yes. We provide superbills you can submit to PPO insurance. Many patients get 40–70% back. HSA/FSA funds can also be used. We'll provide all necessary documentation for reimbursement.
What exactly am I paying for?
You're paying for comprehensive, personalized care that typically isn't available elsewhere: 90-minute consultations, genetic testing interpretation, careful medication selection, and ongoing support—all from a board-certified physician who takes time to understand your full picture.
Are labs and genetic testing included?
Labs are often covered by insurance when ordered through your PCP. Genetic testing is a separate $399 investment that provides lifelong guidance for medication selection. Both are optional but recommended.

Eligibility & Requirements

Do I need a PCP?
Yes. Every patient must have a primary care provider for general health. We focus specifically on mood, while your PCP manages your broader care. This ensures you have comprehensive medical coverage and someone to handle urgent medical needs.
What if my situation is more complex?
If your needs fall outside our scope (bipolar disorder, schizophrenia, addiction, crisis situations), we'll guide you toward the right level of care—psychiatry, intensive therapy, or crisis support. You won't be left on your own.
Do you prescribe controlled substances?
No. We prescribe antidepressants and safe, non-addictive options. We don't prescribe benzodiazepines (like Xanax), stimulants (like Adderall), or sleep medications like Ambien.

About Genetic Testing

How does genetic testing help with depression treatment?
Your genes affect how you metabolize medications. Testing reveals which antidepressants your body processes normally, slowly, or quickly—helping predict which will work best and at what dose. This reduces trial-and-error and side effects.
Is genetic testing required?
No, it's optional. However, about 80% of our patients choose it because it provides valuable, lifelong guidance for medication selection—useful not just for depression but any future medication needs.
How is the test done?
It's a simple cheek swab you do at home. We mail you the kit, you swab your cheek, and mail it back. Results are available in 7-10 days.
Will genetic testing guarantee my medication works?
No treatment is guaranteed, but genetic testing significantly improves our odds of selecting an effective medication on the first or second try, rather than the fourth or fifth.

Getting Started

How quickly can I get an appointment?
Most patients can be seen within 3-5 business days. After completing the fit check and any needed labs, you'll have access to our scheduling system to choose a time that works for you.
What happens in the first visit?
The 90-minute initial consultation includes: comprehensive mood assessment, complete medical history review, discussion of your goals, explanation of treatment options (including genetic testing), and creation of a personalized treatment plan.
How often are follow-ups?
Typically: Week 2 (brief check-in), Week 4 (full follow-up), Week 8 (optimization), then monthly for 3 months, then quarterly. We adjust based on your needs and progress.
Can I cancel or pause treatment?
Yes. There are no long-term contracts. You can pause or stop treatment at any time. We just ask for 24-hour notice for appointment cancellations.
